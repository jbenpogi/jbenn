About Seoul

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a vibrant metropolis where ancient traditions and cutting-edge innovations harmoniously coexist. With a population of over 10 million, it's one of Asia's most dynamic cities, boasting a rich 2,000-year history alongside ultramodern architecture and technology.

From royal palaces and traditional markets to towering skyscrapers and digital districts, Seoul offers an unparalleled blend of old and new. The Han River runs through the city, with scenic pathways perfect for cycling or evening strolls while enjoying the iconic skyline.

Visitors can experience world-class shopping in Myeongdong, explore the artsy streets of Hongdae, or find serenity in Buddhist temples nestled among modern buildings. With its efficient transportation, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, Seoul welcomes millions of travelers each year.

Must-See Attractions

Gyeongbokgung Palace

This royal palace is a center of Korean history and culture. Visitors can witness the vibrant changing of the guard ceremony, explore the National Folk Museum for deeper cultural immersion, and even try on traditional Korean dress (hanbok) for photos and a more authentic experience

Bukchon Hanok Village

offers an immersive experience of traditional Korean lifestyle. Walking through its narrow alleys lined with restored hanok (traditional Korean houses), visitors can appreciate historical architecture and participate in cultural activities such as crafts workshops or tea ceremonies

Hongdae

Things To Do in Seoul

Food Tours

Explore Seoul's vibrant culinary scene with guided food tours through traditional markets. Sample iconic dishes like bibimbap, Korean BBQ, and street food delicacies.

Cultural Performances

Experience traditional Korean music, dance, and martial arts performances at majestic venues like Gyeongbokgung Palace and the Korea House.

Shopping Districts

Discover unique shops in Insadong for traditional crafts, explore luxury brands in Gangnam, or experience the vibrant youth culture and fashion in Hongdae.

Hiking Adventures

Explore the charming Bukchon Hanok Village or hike along Seoul's historic city walls, offering breathtaking views of traditional architecture and modern skylines.

Weather & Best Time

Spring (March-May) and Fall (Sept-Nov) are ideal with mild temperatures and beautiful scenery. Summer (June-Aug) is hot and humid with occasional rain, while Winter (Dec-Feb) is cold with occasional snow.

Language Tips

While English is widely used in tourist areas, learning basic Korean phrases is appreciated. Most signs are in both Korean and English. Download a translation app for ease of communication.

Getting Around

Transportation

Get a T-money card for convenient travel on subway, bus, and taxi. The extensive subway system has English signs and announcements. Airport express trains connect directly to the city center.

Essential Info

Travel Basics

Voltage: 220V (bring adapters). Currency: Korean Won (KRW). Free WiFi widely available. Many stores accept credit cards, but carry cash for traditional markets and street food.

Culture

Local Etiquette

Bow slightly when greeting. Remove shoes before entering homes or certain traditional restaurants. Using two hands to give or receive items shows respect. Tipping is not expected.

Planning

Peak Seasons

Cherry blossom season (early April) and autumn foliage (late October) are peak tourist times. Book accommodations early for these periods and major festivals like Chuseok or Seollal.
